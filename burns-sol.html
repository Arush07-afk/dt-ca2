Emergency Solution - Burns

1

Assess the Situation

A first-degree burn is a minor burn, but a second-degree burn will have further damage to the skin's layers. Second-degree burns will also have blistering, pain, redness, and bleeding.

2

Cool the skin

Place the burned skin under cool running water for 20 minutes. This should help to lower the temperature of your skin. If you don't want to stand next to running water for that long, fill a bowl with cool water and soak the burn in the bowl.

3

Ice the burn if you still feel pain

If you're still feeling pain after cooling the burned area with water, apply ice. Be sure to wrap a washcloth or paper towels around the ice pack to create a barrier. Press the wrapped ice, ice pack, or even a bag of frozen vegetables, against the burn.

4

Apply an antibiotic and wrap the burn if blisters develop

By this point, you should be feeling relief from the pain. You should only need to dress the burn if it's developing a blister (making it a second-degree burn). Dress the burn by simply patting the burned area dry. Generously cover the burn with a topical antibiotic, like Neosporin, and cover it with clean gauze.

Disclaimer: These steps are solely for educational purposes, in case of any emergency, contact the helpline and always seek professional medical help. Do not rely solely on this guide for life-threatening emergencies.
