Emergency Solution - Drug Overdose

1

Look For Symptoms

There are many symptoms of drug overdose, which include:

  • Change in body temperature (sweating or overheating; shivering or drop in body temperature)
  • Heart rate changing drastically
  • Chest pains
  • Difficulty breathing
  • Abdominal cramps
  • Dizziness
  • Seizures

2

Stay close to them, and always keep an eye on them

Someone who has overdosed can go in and out of consciousness. Try to keep the patient awake

3

Make sure they do not eat or drink

If you do not know what they have taken, or even if you do, it may not be safe to put other chemicals in the body, as this can have a negative or even dangerous reaction.

4

Know what to do if the person is having a seizure

In some cases, an overdose can cause a person to have a seizure, which can be doubly scary if you've never dealt with either situation before. However, the steps for dealing with someone's seizures are actually fairly simple in nature.

5

Get information on what substance the person might have taken

If the person is conscious and able to communicate, try to get information on what they overdosed on. This will help the doctors or paramedics to give the appropriate treatment without wasting any time.

Disclaimer: These steps are solely for educational purposes, in case of any emergency, contact the helpline and always seek professional medical help. Do not rely solely on this guide for life-threatening emergencies.
